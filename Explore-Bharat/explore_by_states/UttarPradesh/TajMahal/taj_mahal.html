About Kumbhalgarh

Kumbhalgarh, a majestic fortress nestled in the rugged terrain of the Aravalli Range in Rajasthan, India, stands as a testament to the rich history and architectural brilliance of the region. Built in the 15th century by Rana Kumbha, a revered ruler of the Mewar Kingdom, the fortress of Kumbhalgarh is renowned for its formidable walls, stretching over 38 kilometers and encircling the hill on which it stands. This imposing structure served not only as a symbol of strength and power but also as a haven for the rulers and residents of the kingdom during times of conflict.

Perched atop the hill, Kumbhalgarh Fort offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, with its walls rising high against the backdrop of the expansive Aravalli landscape. Within the fortress, visitors can explore a treasure trove of historical and architectural wonders, including intricately carved temples, grand palaces, and other structures that speak volumes about the artistic and cultural heritage of the region.

Architecture

Kumbhalgarh Fort is renowned for its massive walls, which are considered to be the second-longest continuous wall after the Great Wall of China. The walls extend over 36 kilometers and are 15 feet thick. The fortifications are punctuated by numerous bastions and watchtowers.

Apart from its architectural and historical significance, Kumbhalgarh Fort holds cultural importance as well. It was the birthplace of Maharana Pratap, one of the greatest warriors of Mewar, who fought against the Mughals.

How to Get There

By Road

Kumbhalgarh is well-connected by road to major cities like Udaipur (about 85 km), Jodhpur (about 160 km), and Ahmedabad (about 375 km). State-run buses, as well as private taxis, are available from these cities to Kumbhalgarh.

By Train

The nearest railway station to Kumbhalgarh is Falna Railway Station, located at a distance of about 85 km. It is well-connected to major cities like Ahmedabad, Jaipur, and Delhi. From Falna, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Kumbhalgarh.

By Air

The nearest airport to Kumbhalgarh is Maharana Pratap Airport in Udaipur, located at a distance of about 110 km. This airport is well-connected to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Jaipur. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Kumbhalgarh.
