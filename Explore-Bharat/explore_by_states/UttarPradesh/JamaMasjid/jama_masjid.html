Jama Masjid

HISTORY

According to accounts in the official court chronicle, Shahjahannama, the Jama Masjid originated from an imperial decree by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in the year 1637. Shah Jahan ordered that a new mosque be built in Agra, situated alongside a new chowk (plaza) in front of the Agra Fort's Delhi gate. Shah Jahan's eldest daughter, the princess Jahanara Begum, requested permission from the emperor to personally endow the new mosque, which was granted.

TOURIST ATTRACTIONS

The Jama Masjid of Agra is an early example of imperial city-mosques endowed during the reign of Shah Jahan.It is primarily built of red sandstone, with white marble used intermittently. The mosque covers an area of 1200 square metres, and is elevated above ground level by a raised podium. Decoration on the mosque structure consists of reliefs and mouldings, executed in sandstone with marble inlays. The mosque's plan and elevation are similar to that of the Wazir Khan Mosque, another Mughal mosque of Shah Jahan, built a decade prior in Lahore.

TRANSPORT GUIDE

How to Get There

By Road

One common bus station nearby is the Red Fort Bus Stop, which is about a 5-10 minute walk from Jama Masjid.

By Train

The nearest railway station to Jama Masjid is the Old Delhi Railway Station, also known as Delhi Junction. It is one of the major railway stations in Delhi and is located approximately 1.5 to 2 kilometers away from Jama Masjid

By Air

The nearest airport to Jama Masjid in Old Delhi, India, is the Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL). It is located in the southwestern part of Delhi, approximately 15 to 20 kilometers away from Jama Masjid
