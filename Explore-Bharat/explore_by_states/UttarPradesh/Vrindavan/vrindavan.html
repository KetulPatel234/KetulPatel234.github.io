Vrindavan

HISTORY

The town of Vrindavan in Mathura District of Uttar Pradesh is as relevant to Hindus of India as Krishna is to the great Indian epic, the Mahabharata. Its religious and mythological connotations are central to the worship of Krishna in the pantheon of Hindu gods. A precocious child, a trusted friend, a performer of miracles, a romantic lover and a saviour to all; Vrindavan is deeply associated with the life and times of Krishna and the tales woven around his life.

TOURIST ATTRACTIONS

Vrindavan is considered to be a sacred place for Vaisnavism tradition of Hinduism. It is believed that Krishna spent part of his childhood in this city. The other prominent areas surrounding Vrindavan are Govardhana, Gokul, Nandgaon, Barsana, Mathura and Bhandirvan.

TRANSPORT GUIDE

How to Get There

By Road

The nearest bus station to Ram Mandir in Vrindavan, Uttar Pradesh, India, is the Vrindavan Bus Stand. From there, you can take a local transport option like a rickshaw or taxi to reach the Ram Mandir

By Train

The nearest railway station to Vrindavan is Mathura Junction Railway Station (station code: MTJ). Mathura Junction is one of the major railway stations in Uttar Pradesh and is well-connected to various cities across India. From Mathura Junction, you can easily reach Vrindavan by hiring a taxi, taking a bus, or using other local transportation options. The distance between Mathura Junction Railway Station and Vrindavan is approximately 15 kilometers

By Air

The nearest airport to Vrindavan is the Agra Airport, officially known as Pandit Deen Dayal Upadhyay Airport (IATA code: AGR). It's located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, 65 kilometers from Vrindavan
